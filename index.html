January 21, 2029. The world seemed to hold together, barely. Wars raged elsewhere, governments argued, but in France, the streets were growing dangerous and the prisons overcrowded. Amid the chaos, Sel operated in the shadows. Psychologist, she claimed to be. In reality, she was not a real therapist. She had bought her diploma on the dark net and used it to collect human secrets. People confessed their darkest acts, murders, betrayals, obsessions, while she smiled, noting every word, calculating every reaction. Each confession had a price, and she sold them to anonymous buyers without concern for how they would be used. Sel had no real name, no official identity. Just Sel. She had learned early that trust was a lie. Every person who had been close to her had betrayed her, used her, or tried to destroy her. She stopped caring. She listened, she smiled, she profited. The suffering of others became her currency, their desperation her advantage. Every night she returned home, her pockets full, her conscience silent, sipping Kavod as she planned her next move. She was not there to help anyone. She was there to be remembered, to carve a name into history, even if it was for the wrong reasons. Eventually, someone discovered that Sel was a fake therapist and had been selling sensitive information. It did not matter who revealed it. The truth came out, and the law caught up with her. Sel was arrested and sent to jail
